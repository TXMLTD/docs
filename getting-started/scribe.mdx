Scribe is TextMine's template authoring module. It lets you turn any standard document — a contract, policy, or agreement — into a smart template that the rest of your organisation can use to generate correctly configured documents by filling in a form, without needing to edit the underlying document directly.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Scribe">
    Navigate to the Scribe section from the left-hand sidebar. If you do not yet have a Scribe workspace, follow the prompt to create one. Scribe workspaces are linked to your organisation's TextMine account.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a new template">
    Click **New template** and either:

    - Upload an existing document (PDF or Word) to use as the base.
    - Start from a blank document and write the content directly in Scribe's editor.

    Give the template a clear, descriptive name — this is what your team will see when they search for templates in Legislate.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add fields to your template">
    Fields are the variable parts of your document — names, dates, values, and choices that will differ from one document to the next. To add a field:

    1. Highlight the text in your template that should become a variable.
    2. Select **Create field** from the toolbar that appears.
    3. Choose the field type: **Text**, **Date**, **Number**, **Yes/No**, or **Choice**.
    4. Give the field a label and, optionally, a description to guide the person filling it in.

    Repeat this for every variable in the document.

    <Tip>Write clear field descriptions. When team members fill in the form in Legislate, your descriptions appear as inline help text, reducing back-and-forth questions.</Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure field rules (optional)">
    You can make fields conditional — for example, showing a payment terms field only when a specific billing option is selected. Use the **Rules** panel on a field to set conditions based on other field values.

    <Info>Conditional fields keep the form clean for users and prevent irrelevant data from appearing in generated documents.</Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Publish to your team">
    When your template is ready, click **Publish**. You will be prompted to select which Legislate team should have access to the template. Once published, team members can find the template in Legislate and create new documents from it by completing the form you have built.

    <Note>You can update a published template at any time. Documents already created from the previous version are not affected — they retain the version that was in place when they were generated.</Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>
