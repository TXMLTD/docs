Custom alerts help you stay on top of important document dates without having to manually check each agreement. You can set up alerts for events like an upcoming expiry or a renewal decision deadline, choose who receives them, and schedule exactly when the notification should arrive. Alerts are delivered by email to the people you specify.

## Alert types

Legislate supports four types of custom alerts:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Expiry" icon="calendar-xmark">
    Get notified when a document is approaching its expiry date, so you can act before the agreement lapses.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Notice" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    Receive an alert within the notice period of a document — useful for agreements that require advance notice before termination or renewal.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Renewal" icon="rotate">
    Be reminded when a document is due for renewal, giving you time to renegotiate or extend terms.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Review" icon="magnifying-glass-check">
    Set a review reminder to prompt a periodic check of the document's terms and continued relevance.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How to create an alert

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the Alerts tab">
    In the Legislate sidebar, click on the **Alerts** tab in the top-left area. This opens your alerts dashboard, where you can see and manage all existing alerts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Create alert">
    Click the **Create alert** button. You'll be guided through a short setup flow.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the team">
    Choose the Legislate team that contains the document you want to create an alert for.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the document">
    Within that team, select the specific document this alert applies to.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the alert type">
    Select one of the four alert types: Expiry, Notice, Renewal, or Review.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Specify recipients">
    Add the people who should receive this alert. Alerts can be sent to multiple recipients.

    Alerts can only be sent to users who already have access to the document. If someone doesn't appear in the recipient list, check that they've been granted document access first.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the delivery time and day">
    Choose when the alert should be delivered — specifying both the time of day and the date or schedule that triggers it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the alert">
    Click **Save**. The alert will now appear in your alerts dashboard and will be sent automatically at the scheduled time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Managing existing alerts

All your alerts are listed in the **Alerts** tab. From there you can:

- Review the details of any alert (type, document, recipients, schedule)
- Edit an alert to update its recipients or timing
- Delete an alert that is no longer needed

<Tip>
Setting alerts at the time you create a document — while the relevant dates are fresh — is the most reliable way to avoid missing a notice period or renewal window.
</Tip>
