Legislate makes it easy to generate correctly structured documents without ever touching the underlying template text. When a Scribe template has been published to your team, all you need to do is fill in a form — Legislate handles the rest, producing a complete document ready for review and signature.

## Before you start

Make sure a Scribe template for the document type you need has been published to your Legislate team. If you don't see the template you need when going through the creation flow, ask your Scribe administrator to publish it to your team.

## Create a new document

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Create">
    From within your Legislate team, click the **Create** button. You'll be prompted to provide a name for the folder where this document will be stored. You can also place the document inside an existing folder if one already exists.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the template and version">
    Choose the document type you want to create, then select the Scribe template version you want to use. Different versions may be available if the template has been updated and republished over time. Click **Continue** when you're ready.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose whether to copy previous terms">
    Legislate gives you the option to copy terms over from a previous document of the same type, or to start from scratch. Starting from scratch presents you with a blank form; copying from a previous document pre-fills the form with values from that earlier document, which you can then adjust.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the Scribe form">
    Complete the form with the details specific to this document instance. The questions and fields in the form were defined by the Scribe template author. Answer each question carefully — your answers will be inserted directly into the document text.

    If the template uses conditional logic, answering certain questions (particularly radio fields) will determine which sections of the document are included.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and preview">
    Click **Save** once the form is complete. You can then review the fully rendered document in the **Preview** tab to confirm everything looks correct before proceeding.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Add parties to the document

After filling in the form, go to the **People** tab to add the parties involved in the document.

A party can be either a company or an individual:

- **Company**: When specifying a company as a party, you must also designate a signatory — the individual authorised to sign on the company's behalf.
- **Individual**: Add the person's details directly as the party.

<Note>
Documents cannot be signed until all required parties have been added and terms have been set. Check the **Checklist** tab to see which steps are still pending.
</Note>

## Add collaborators, witnesses, and supporting signatories

Beyond the primary parties, you can give access to additional people using the **Other people** button on the People tab:

- **Collaborators**: People who need to view or work on the document but are not signing parties.
- **Witnesses**: Individuals required to witness signatures on the document.
- **Supporting signatories**: Additional people whose signature is needed in a supporting capacity.

Once parties are in place and the document is complete, you're ready to move to the signing stage. See [Signing Documents](/legislate/signing-documents) for the next steps.
