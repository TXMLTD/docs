Once a document has been filled in and all required parties have been added, Legislate allows you to initiate the signing process directly within the platform. You can draw a signature freehand or upload an existing one, and Legislate tracks the signature status of all parties so everyone stays informed.

## When can you sign?

Signing becomes available only after two conditions are met:

1. The document terms have been fully set (the form has been completed and saved).
2. Parties on all sides have been added to the document.

To check whether a document is ready to sign, open the **Checklist** tab. When the **Review and Sign** item is ready, you'll see it become active. You can either click **Review and Sign** directly from the checklist, or click the **Sign** icon elsewhere in the document view to proceed.

<Note>
If the signing option isn't yet available, check the Checklist tab to see which steps are still outstanding. Typically this means a party hasn't been added or the form hasn't been fully completed.
</Note>

## Sign the document

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the signing screen">
    Click **Review and Sign** in the Checklist tab, or click the **Sign** icon from the document view. This opens the signing interface where you can review the document one final time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Sign contract">
    Once you're ready to sign, click the **Sign contract** button.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Draw or upload your signature">
    You have two options for adding your signature:

    - **Draw**: Use your mouse, trackpad, or touchscreen to draw your signature in the signature area.
    - **Upload**: Upload an image of your existing signature.

    Choose whichever method you prefer and confirm to apply your signature to the document.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Tracking signatures

After you sign, Legislate records your signature in the **Signatures** tab. You can return to this tab at any time to see the current signature status for all parties on the document.

When you sign, the counterparty receives a notification letting them know the document is awaiting their signature.

## Cancelling a signature

If you've signed but the other party hasn't yet, you can cancel your signature:

1. Go to the **Signatures** tab.
2. Click the **bin (delete) icon** next to your name in the signatures list.

<Warning>
You can only cancel your own signature, and only if the counterparty has not yet signed. Once all parties have signed, the document is fully executed and signatures cannot be removed.
</Warning>
