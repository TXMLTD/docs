Advanced Search gives you a fast, flexible way to find documents based on what's inside them — not just their names or dates. Filters are generated dynamically from the actual data in your documents, so you're always working with real, relevant criteria. This makes it straightforward to answer questions like "show me all contracts expiring this quarter" or "which employment agreements are for part-time roles" without manually opening each document.

## Where to find Advanced Search

Advanced Search is located directly below the main search bar in Legislate. You don't need to navigate to a separate screen — scroll down from the search bar and the filter panel appears.

## How filters work

Filters in Advanced Search are generated dynamically based on the data contained in your documents. This means:

- You only see filter options that actually exist across your document library.
- If no documents contain a particular field value, that option won't appear as a filter.
- As you apply filters, the document list and the remaining filter options both update in real time to reflect what still matches.

This live narrowing makes it easy to drill down through a large document library without knowing exactly what you're looking for ahead of time.

## Applying filters and searching

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Advanced Search">
    In Legislate, look below the search bar for the Advanced Search filter panel. Click to expand it if it isn't already visible.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a filter">
    Choose one or more filter values from the options shown. The document list immediately updates to show only documents that match. The other filter options also update to reflect what's available within the remaining results.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add more filters to narrow results">
    Continue selecting additional filters to refine the results further. Each new filter narrows the document list to the intersection of all selected criteria.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the matching documents">
    Once you've applied your filters, review the documents shown in the list. Click any document to open it and inspect it in detail.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Exporting results as a CSV

Once you've filtered down to the documents you're interested in, you can export the results for reporting, audits, or sharing with stakeholders.

Click the **Export** button (or CSV export option) in the search results area. Legislate will generate a CSV file containing:

- The documents that match your current filter criteria
- The corresponding field values for each document

<Tip>
The exported CSV makes document audits fast and repeatable. You can share a snapshot of your document data with finance, compliance, or management teams without needing to give them direct access to Legislate.
</Tip>

<Info>
The fields that appear in the CSV export correspond to the Scribe template fields defined by your template author. If a field you expect to see in the export is missing, it may not have been included in the template.
</Info>
