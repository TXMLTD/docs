Scribe is TextMine's template creation and management tool. It lets your legal or compliance team define reusable document templates — complete with fillable fields, conditional logic, and tracked-change collaboration — and then publish those templates to the rest of the business so anyone can generate a correctly structured document by simply filling in a form.

## How Scribe works

The workflow in Scribe follows a clear path from a blank template to a signed document.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a template">
    Start by creating a new template document. You choose which folder to store it in and assign a document type (for example, NDA or Employment Contract). You can create new folders and types or pick from existing ones.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Draft and insert fields">
    Write your document in the Scribe editor, formatting text as you would in any word processor. When you reach a part of the document that should vary between instances — such as a party name or a start date — highlight that text and convert it into a field. Fields appear in a panel on the right and form the basis of the form users will complete later.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add conditional logic">
    For sections of the document that should appear or disappear depending on the user's choices, you can attach conditional logic to radio-type fields. This lets one template cover multiple scenarios without duplicating content.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Collaborate and review">
    Invite teammates to review your draft. Switch between Edit mode and Request mode to either make live edits or submit tracked suggestions. Comments and flags help surface discussion points. All open items must be resolved before publishing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Publish to Legislate">
    When your template is ready, publish it and choose which Legislate teams should have access. From that point, team members can create document instances by filling in the form you designed — without needing to touch the template itself.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Key capabilities

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Field-driven forms" icon="input-text">
    Every variable in your template becomes a form field. Users filling out a document only see the questions you defined — they never edit the underlying template text directly.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Conditional logic" icon="code-branch">
    Use radio fields to control which sections of a document render. One template can produce multiple document variations based on a user's answers.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Collaborative drafting" icon="users">
    Edit mode and Request mode let teams collaborate with tracked suggestions, comments, and flags — similar to track-changes workflows you already know.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Version-controlled publishing" icon="tag">
    Published templates are versioned. You can republish to additional teams, duplicate a template to make edits, and track all published versions from the Scribe dashboard.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How Scribe connects to Legislate

Scribe and Legislate are two sides of the same document lifecycle. Scribe is where templates are built and maintained by your document owners or legal team. Legislate is where everyone else — HR, sales, operations — creates documents from those templates.

<Note>
Legislate users do not have access to Scribe by default. A Scribe editor must publish a template to the relevant Legislate team before users there can create documents from it.
</Note>

Once a template is published, Legislate users see a simple form driven entirely by the fields you configured in Scribe. They fill in the answers, add parties, and initiate signing — all without touching the template language itself.
