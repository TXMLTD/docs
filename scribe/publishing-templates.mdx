Publishing a template is the final step in Scribe. It makes your template available to Legislate users so they can generate documents from it by filling in a form. You control exactly which Legislate teams get access, so you can roll out templates to the right groups without making them universally available.

## Prerequisites before publishing

Before you can publish, your template must be completely clean — no open discussions or pending changes.

<Warning>
You cannot publish a template that has unresolved comments, requests, or flags. Resolve all open items in the Comments, Requests, and Flags tabs before attempting to publish.
</Warning>

If the Publish button is greyed out or unavailable, check each of the three tabs in the right-hand panel for outstanding items.

## How to publish a template

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the template">
    In the Scribe dashboard, open the template you want to publish.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Preview the document">
    Before publishing, use the preview to verify that the template renders correctly. This is a good final check to confirm fields are positioned correctly and conditional sections behave as expected.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Legislate teams">
    Click **Publish** in the top ribbon. You'll be prompted to choose which Legislate teams should have access to this template. You can select one or more teams.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm and publish">
    Confirm your selection. The template is now published and will be available to users in the selected teams when they go to create a new document in Legislate.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Tracking published templates

After publishing, you can monitor all published templates from the **Published** tab of the Scribe dashboard. This view shows you which templates are live and which teams can access them.

## Managing a published template

Once a template is published, you have two management options accessible from the **three-dot menu** next to any template in the Published tab.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Republish" icon="paper-plane">
    Use **Republish** to grant access to additional Legislate teams without changing the template content. This is useful when you want to expand availability after an initial rollout.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Duplicate" icon="copy">
    Use **Duplicate** to create an editable copy of the published template. Because published templates are locked, duplication is the correct path if you need to make content changes — edit the duplicate, then publish it as a new version.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
Legislate users do not have access to Scribe by default. A published template is only visible in Legislate to users who belong to a team you explicitly selected during the publish step.
</Note>

## What happens in Legislate after publishing

Once published, the template becomes available to users in the chosen Legislate teams via the **Create** button. Users select the document type and template version, fill in the form you designed, and generate a completed document — without ever needing to edit the template text directly.
