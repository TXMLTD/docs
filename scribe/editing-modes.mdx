Scribe is designed for collaborative drafting. Multiple people can work on the same template simultaneously, contributing edits, suggestions, comments, and flags at any stage. Two distinct editing modes control how changes are recorded, and a resolution workflow ensures that all open items are addressed before a template can be published.

## Switching between modes

The mode selector appears in the top-left ribbon of the editor, between the **Publish** and **Save** buttons.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Edit mode">
    Edit mode lets you make direct, live changes to the template. Any text you add, remove, or reformat is applied immediately without being tracked as a suggestion. Use Edit mode when you are an authorised editor making final or authoritative changes.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Request mode">
    Request mode tracks every change you make as a suggested edit — similar to "Track Changes" in Microsoft Word. Each suggestion appears as a request in the right-hand panel, where other collaborators can review and act on it.

    Requests are grouped under the **Requests** tab. You can filter between open and closed requests using the tab controls.

    **Important:** You cannot accept your own requests. Another collaborator with edit access must approve or reject changes you submit in Request mode.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Comments

Comments let you leave notes on specific sections of the template without proposing a wording change.

To leave a comment:
1. Highlight the relevant portion of text in the editor.
2. Enter your comment in the toolbar that appears.

Comments are grouped under the **Comments** tab in the right-hand panel. You can switch between the Comments, Requests, and Flags tabs by clicking on each tab label.

## Flags

Flags are a way to escalate a section of the draft to the wider team — for example, to signal that a clause needs specialist review before the template is finalised.

To add a flag:
1. Highlight the text that needs flagging.
2. Select the **Flag** option from the toolbar.

Flagged sections appear under the **Flags** tab in the right-hand panel.

## Inviting collaborators

To bring other people into the review process, click the **pen icon** in the top-right corner of the editor. You can invite teammates to view or collaborate on the template from there.

## Resolving requests, comments, and flags

All open requests, comments, and flags must be resolved before you can publish a template.

<Warning>
Scribe will block publishing if there are any unresolved requests, comments, or flags. Work through the open items in each tab and resolve them before attempting to publish.
</Warning>

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Resolving a request">
    Open requests appear in the **Requests** tab. Any collaborator with edit access (other than the person who submitted the request) can accept or reject each suggestion. Accepted changes are applied to the document; rejected ones are discarded. Both outcomes close the request.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Resolving a comment">
    Comments can be resolved once the discussion they prompted has been addressed. Click the resolve option on the comment in the **Comments** tab to close it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Resolving a flag">
    Flags are resolved in the same way as comments. Once the flagged issue has been handled, mark the flag as resolved in the **Flags** tab.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
